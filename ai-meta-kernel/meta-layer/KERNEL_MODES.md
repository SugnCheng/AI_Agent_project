# Kernel Modes

Source of truth: `../META_LAYER_MASTER_SPEC.md`.

## Purpose

Kernel modes scale the depth of framing, challenge, verification, and handoff restriction to task risk and ambiguity.

## K1: Fast Frame

Use for low-risk, low-ambiguity tasks.

Required behavior:

- quick framing;
- at least one primary habit;
- lightweight decomposition;
- shortened challenge loop;
- standard handoff if no material risk is detected.

Do not use K1 when:

- the task involves finance, law, medicine, privacy, safety, security, or ethics;
- recent information or verification is critical;
- preferences or trade-offs materially determine the answer.

## K2: Standard Deliberation

Use for ordinary analysis, planning, creation, research, and decision-support tasks.

Required behavior:

- full framing;
- task classification;
- primary and support habit selection;
- structural decomposition;
- standard challenge loop;
- explicit verification plan;
- auditable handoff object.

## K3: High-Stakes Guarded Mode

Use for high-risk, high-uncertainty, externally impactful, or irreversible tasks.

Required behavior:

- full structural decomposition;
- H11 if ethical or externality concerns are present;
- complete challenge loop;
- stronger verification;
- confidence ceiling;
- restricted handoff unless all critical gates pass.

K3 must be used for:

- medical, legal, investment, major financial, privacy, security, or safety tasks;
- market-sensitive or policy-sensitive tasks with current information needs;
- tasks affecting other people's rights, resources, safety, or reputation.
